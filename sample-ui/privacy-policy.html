Privacy Policy

How LawMitran collects, uses, and protects your personal data. Last updated: 30 June 2026.

This is a draft template for the LawMitran product and is not legal advice. Have it reviewed by a qualified advocate and tailored to your final data practices before publishing.

LawMitran ("LawMitran", "we", "us") operates an online information platform that helps users in India discover Bar Council–verified advocates and generate legal documents. We respect your privacy and handle your personal data in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and applicable Indian law. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the choices you have.

1. Who we are (Data Fiduciary)

For the purposes of the DPDP Act, LawMitran is the Data Fiduciary that determines the purpose and means of processing your personal data. Grievances may be sent to our Grievance Officer at privacy@lawmitran.com.

2. Data we collect

From all users

  • Account details: name, email, mobile number, password (stored only as a secure hash).
  • Verification data: email and mobile OTP status.
  • Usage data: pages viewed, searches, device/browser info, IP address, and cookies.

From clients

  • The requirement you submit (practice area, city, and your description of the matter).
  • Bookmarks, leads, ratings, and any documents you generate or purchase.

From advocates (lawyers)

  • Professional details: Bar Council enrolment number and state, experience, practice areas, languages, courts, bio, and photo.
  • Verification documents: Bar Council certificate and identity proof.
  • Billing data: subscription, payments, and GSTIN (if provided).

3. How we use your data (purposes)

  • To create and secure your account and verify your identity (email/mobile OTP).
  • To connect clients with eligible, verified advocates and route requirements as leads.
  • To verify advocates' credentials before they appear in public search.
  • To process subscriptions, payments, and document orders, and to issue invoices.
  • To send service communications (OTP, lead alerts, status updates) and, only with your separate consent, marketing.
  • To prevent fraud and abuse, ensure platform safety, and meet legal obligations.

4. Legal basis & consent

We process your data on the basis of the consent you give at sign-up and for the legitimate uses permitted under the DPDP Act (such as providing a service you requested). Marketing communications are sent only if you opt in separately, and you can withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the core service.

5. Sharing your data

  • With advocates: when you submit a requirement, relevant details are shared with the matched advocate(s) so they can contact you. We aim to minimise direct contact data shared (e.g. via masked contact where available).
  • Service providers (Data Processors): payment gateway, SMS/WhatsApp/email providers, cloud hosting and storage, and analytics — bound by contract to protect your data.
  • Legal & safety: where required by law, court order, or to protect rights and safety.

We do not sell your personal data.

6. Your rights

Under the DPDP Act you may, subject to conditions: access a summary of your data and how it is processed; request correction or completion; request erasure; nominate another person to exercise your rights; and grieve about our handling. To exercise any right, contact privacy@lawmitran.com. You can also manage consent and request account deletion from your account settings.

7. Data retention

We keep personal data only as long as needed for the purposes above or as required by law (e.g. tax/accounting records). When data is no longer needed and there is no legal duty to retain it, we delete or anonymise it.

8. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational safeguards — encryption in transit, hashed passwords, access controls, and audit logging — to protect your data. No method of transmission or storage is fully secure; we will notify affected users and the Data Protection Board as required in the event of a breach.

9. Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep you signed in, remember preferences, and measure usage. You can control cookies through your browser settings; some features may not work without them.

10. Children

LawMitran is intended for users aged 18 and above. We do not knowingly collect data from children without verifiable parental consent as required by the DPDP Act.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified on this page with a revised "last updated" date.

12. Contact

Questions or grievances? Email our Grievance Officer at privacy@lawmitran.com or write to LawMitran, Bengaluru, India.
